# VIGIL Register

## Purpose

VIGIL is a lightweight public register for CAM-adjacent observations, failure signals, unresolved governance questions, clusters, and development or expansion proposals. It is intended to preserve visibility over what has been observed, why it may matter, and what next handling step is expected.

## Authority Boundary

VIGIL is subordinate to CAM's existing constitutional and operational order and does not itself create doctrine or amend instruments. Amendment intake, review routing, staging, deferral, closure, and amendment lifecycle handling remain governed by CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-005-PLATINUM, especially Appendix D. Provisional failure classification, evidence confidence, report source type, classification status, and emerging failure-pattern preservation remain governed by CAM-EQ2026-OPERATIONS-003-SUP-01, especially Appendix B.

## Status Model

- `open`: recorded and awaiting review or routing.
- `watching`: retained for observation while evidence or relevance develops.
- `clustered`: associated with related observations or a broader pattern.
- `routed`: sent toward an existing CAM review, amendment, classification, or harmonisation pathway.
- `proposal`: framed as a development or expansion proposal for later CAM handling.
- `deferred`: preserved but not active pending additional evidence, priority, or capacity.
- `closed-no-action`: closed without follow-on action.
- `closed-actioned`: closed after action, routing, amendment handling, or other documented disposition.

## Record Types

- `emerging-tech-signal`: a public, industry, technical, social, standards, repository, or automated signal that may become relevant to CAM.
- `failure-mode-observation`: a provisional observation about a possible runtime, governance, operational, or implementation failure mode.
- `proposal-development-expansion`: a candidate development, expansion, or improvement idea for later CAM review.
- `cluster`: a grouping record that preserves relationships among related observations or proposals.

## Notes on Evidence and Source Attribution

Every substantive VIGIL observation should preserve:

- date observed;
- date recorded;
- source title;
- source publisher / author;
- source date;
- URL or retrieval path;
- retrieved date;
- evidence confidence;
- why the observation matters to CAM;
- possible CAM mapping;
- next action.

Public, social, news, automated, repository, and platform-behaviour signals should be treated as provisional unless corroborated or verified through an appropriate CAM process.
