## Audit notes

- `Recovered` means a unique or high-confidence source URL was written back into the record.
- `Partially Recovered` means at least one high-confidence locator was recovered and applied, while some broader or non-unique candidate references remain unapplied.
- `Candidate Sources Identified` means candidate evidence exists but was not applied because confidence was insufficient.
- `Recovery Failed` means repository history and public-source searches did not identify a unique or high-confidence URL.
- `Not Required` means the source is internal-only, already has adequate metadata, or has `source_url_status: "not applicable"` for a valid internal linked-record/governance source.
- `human_review_required` is used where missing, ambiguous, unarchived, or public-user evidence requires manual recovery or verification rather than automated URL invention.
- `automation_safe_metadata_cleanup` is used only for mechanical schema or metadata normalization that does not alter substantive claims.
- No missing external URL was fabricated. Where no unique source could be recovered, the source remains blank and marked with `source_url_status: "missing_url_requires_research"`.
- See `vigil/docs/VIGIL.AuthoringGuidance.md` for Multi Vendor and evidence-recovery authoring guidance.
